A Free Resource · For Parents & Educators

Three minutes that could keep a kid safer at the beach.

A short, clear video built to teach the kids in your life how to read the ocean, before they ever step in it. Watch it together. Share it freely.

Why I Made This

Most kids learn about the ocean too late, or not at all.

Drowning is one of the leading causes of accidental death for children under 14. A lot of those tragedies happen because nobody ever sat the kid down and explained, in plain language, how the ocean actually works, what a rip current looks like, why the lifeguard's flag matters, what to do when a wave is bigger than you expected.

This video is my attempt to fix a small piece of that. It's three minutes, it's visual, and it's designed for a child to watch with a parent, a grandparent, or a teacher.

If you find it useful, the best thing you can do is share it with one other family.

How to Use It

Built for the way real families and classrooms learn.

01

Watch it together

Three minutes is the right length for a child's attention span. Sit next to them. Pause when something raises a question. The conversation matters more than the video.

02

Use it in the car on the way

Heading to the beach this weekend? Cast it from your phone before you go. It primes everyone to spot the lifeguard, find the flags, and pick a safe spot to play.

03

Play it in a classroom or summer camp

Free to use in any educational setting. No permissions needed. If you want a downloadable copy for spotty Wi-Fi, the link below has you covered.

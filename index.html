The Thesis

Every organization is acquiring AI faster than its people can think with it.

The Complexity Gap

AI capability is growing exponentially. Human cognitive capacity is not growing at all. The distance between what your tools can produce and what your people can evaluate is the defining crisis of this decade.

Cognitive Offloading

When people outsource the process of thinking to AI, they don't just save effort. They prevent growth. Harvard research found that elite consultants relying on AI were 19 percentage points less accurate on tasks at the edge of its capabilities.

The Factory Mind

Our education system was designed in 1806 to produce compliant workers. AI now executes compliance faster than any human. The system is mass-producing a cognitive profile that has been structurally obsolete since the first language model could write a legal brief.

Universal Basic Upgrading

AI literacy is horizontal — it spreads across the surface. What organizations and schools actually need is vertical cognitive development: the ability to think at a level where AI makes people more powerful, not more replaceable.

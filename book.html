The Premise

The system you build for AI is the system that builds your people.

Every organization is racing to adopt AI. Most are racing in the wrong direction. They're optimizing for outputs and ignoring the humans who have to evaluate, apply, and lead with what AI produces.

Cognitive Architecture is a framework for the leaders who refuse to let that happen. It draws from cognitive science, organizational design, and field data from 100+ organizations to answer one question: how do you design AI systems that make your people sharper instead of more obsolete?

This is not a book about prompts. This is a book about the architecture of how humans and AI think together, and how leaders can design organizations that thrive because of that partnership, not in spite of it.

19% Less accurate Harvard found that elite consultants relying on AI were 19 percentage points less accurate on tasks at the edge of its capabilities. This book explains why, and what to do about it.

Stop identifying with your tool, your degree, or your job title. Start identifying with the value you create between systems. This is the foundational shift the book is engineered to produce.

The Centaur Architecture

Build your own intelligence in Lane 1, then direct the machine's intelligence in Lane 2. The metacognitive practice that separates the Architect from the user.

04

The Antifragile Ego

An internal architecture that grows stronger from disruption. A self-regulating system that treats failure as truth-seeking instead of an attack on self-image. Critical for surviving the next decade.

05

The Horizontal / Vertical Distinction

Why piling on more tools (horizontal) without growing your capacity to evaluate them (vertical) is a faster engine with no steering wheel. And how to invest in the steering instead.

Stop asking, "How do I protect my job?" Start asking, "Where is the arbitrage between the new technology and the old human needs?" That one question is the basis for many of the next big companies.
